  • Area: Event
  • Year: 2020
Redesign of the Palacomieco experience. Project in collaboration with Comieco

The path undertaken for the restyling of PalaComieco stems from the desire to create a formative and educational experience for schools. To make them aware of the theme of the importance of recycling, what we wanted to propose is an experience that starts from the school benches and finds its continuation in the Palacomieco. It is in fact primarily to schools and children that we want to address. The reason behind this choice is very clear and defined; as Bruno Munari, the well-known Italian designer who spent so much time with children, says: "Today's children are tomorrow's adults. Let's help them grow up free of stereotypes, let's help them develop all their senses, let's help them become more sensitive, a creative child is a happier child. Working with children becomes interesting because it is, shall we say, becoming. What you learn and see at an early age is something that sticks. And if the theme on which to raise awareness, as in the case of Comieco, is recycling, it is interesting to work with children, because tomorrow they will be adults. Through the primary gesture of taking care, in particular of taking care of a simple cube that is "born" among the school desks and finds its appearance and place during the Palacomieco experience, we want to show children how the simple caring of a small piece of furniture brings value and change in a collective of collectivity.

Team: Veronica Busignani, Marco Gabriele, Marta Monti
